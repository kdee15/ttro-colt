YOUR STEPS TO PERSONAL GROWTH

Lifelong learners will always be successful in their careers.

No matter when or where your journey starts on the path to continual learning, choosing to say 'yes' to learning is your key to owning a fulfilling personal development journey.

Two questions to ask yourself are: 'How can I become a lifelong learner?' and 'What skills do I need to start my personal development journey?'

BECOME SELF-AWARE

Realise that your mental attitude towards growth has to change. When you assume your abilities, skills and intelligence are set and can’t be changed, you have a fixed mindset. If you hold onto the belief that there is nothing more to learn and that you can’t change, your view of success becomes narrow and limited. You focus on avoiding failure as much as possible and end up losing your ability to reach your full potential.

ADOPT A GROWTH MINDSET

With a growth mindset, you are able to embrace change willingly and allow development to become a positive influence in your life. You will see your skills, abilities and creativity as dynamic and fluid. In turn, you will accept all opportunities as a springboard to learning and an entry point into greater personal development and improvement.

BECOME A LIFELONG LEARNER

Choosing to embrace a growth mindset as a basic belief gives you the power to become a lifelong learner. It puts the title deeds for ownership of your personal development journey into your own hands and becomes your blueprint to personal mastery.

Turn yourself into a personal-mastery guru by power-boosting your personal development journey.

Here are some tools to help you focus on how you behave:

1. BE READY, WILLING AND SELF-AWARE

Always be ready and willing to make a change. Take ownership of your self-awareness. Use it to manage the impact that certain situations may have on you and others.

2. IDENTIFY YOUR SPECIFIC INTENTION

Identify the specific behaviours that you want to develop. Work with your manager or coach to develop an effective goal to achieve this change.

3. ASK FOR HELP

Share your goal with others who will support your development and help you to maintain your accountability for this change.

4. GET THEIR FEEDBACK

Proactively seek feedback from those who will give you both honest and constructive feedback on the progress of your behaviour changes.

5. LEARN FROM THEIR FEEDBACK

Reflect on this feedback and then discuss it with your manager or coach. Agree what changes need to be made to your original goal and adjust it accordingly.

6. MONITOR AND MAINTAIN

Monitor your progress by checking in regularly with your manager or coach. Celebrate success at every step to personal growth!
