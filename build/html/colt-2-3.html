YOUR STEPS TO PERSONAL GROWTH

Lifelong learners will always be successful in their careers.

No matter when or where your journey on the path to continuous learning starts, choosing to say yes to learning is your key to owning a fulfilling personal development journey.

Two questions to ask yourself are how you can become a lifelong learner and what skills do you need to start your personal development journey?

FIRST

Realise that your mental attitude towards growth has to change. When you assume your abilities, skills and intelligence are set and can’t be changed, you have a fixed mindset. If you hold onto the belief that there is nothing more to learn and that you can’t change, your view of success becomes narrow and limited. You focus on avoiding failure as much as possible and end up losing your ability to reach your full potential.

THEN

Adjust from a fixed mindset to a growth mindset. With a growth mindset, though, you are able to willingly embrace change and allow learning and development to become positive influences in your life. You see your skills, abilities and creativity as dynamic and fluid. This means that you can accept failure as a springboard to learn from, and an entry point into greater personal development and improvement.

EMBRACE A GROWTH MINDSET

Choosing to embrace a growth mindset as a basic belief gives you the power to become a lifelong learner. It puts the title deeds of ownership for your personal development journey into your own hands and becomes your blueprint to personal mastery.

Turn yourself into a personal-mastery guru by power boosting your personal development journey.

Here are some tools to help you focus on how you behave:

1. BE READY, WILLING AND SELF-AWARE

Always be ready and willing to make a change. Take ownership of your self-coaching and use your emotional intelligence (EQ) wisely as you communicate your own emotions and deal with the emotional reactions of others around you.

2. IDENTIFY YOUR SPECIFIC INTENTION

Choose a specific behaviour that you want to change, then think about ‘how’ you are going to change it.

3. ASK FOR HELP

Handpick a mentor and people to form your ‘circle of support’ – they will guide and help you. Remember, being able to recognise and admit you need help is an important part of your personal development journey. Don’t waste time doing the wrong thing!

4. GET THEIR FEEDBACK

Ask your circle of support for their objective feedback about your behaviour.

5. LEARN FROM THEIR FEEDBACK

Be resilient in your reactions as you assess the feedback you get. You trust your circle of support, so learn from it as part of your journey to become a self-mastery guru.

6. SO SOMETHING ABOUT IT

Use your circle of support’s feedback wisely. Plan how you’re going to transform your behaviour, then implement your plan. Prioritise and execute - that’s the key!

7. MONITOR AND MAINTAIN

Ask your circle of support for their objective feedback about your behaviour.
