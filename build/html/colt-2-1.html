I'M A COLTIE, NOW WHAT?

You had the interview; you got the job. Now it’s your first day at Colt. A million questions run through your mind. Will I make it? Will I be successful? What first impression will I give to my new colleagues?

CONGRATULATIONS!

The first day at a new job can be difficult, and you have to find ways to fit in quickly, making a good impression is important. These practical steps and tips are here to help you through the first week of your Colt career.

Use them and be surprised at the ease of your transition! Here are some tips to help you along in your first week.

YOUR FIRST WEEK

This week, try to balance the pressure and anxiety. It’s super exciting, although sometimes overwhelming, to meet your new colleagues, learn your new role, and find your way around the office. But, don’t sweat the small stuff and try to take in as much as you possibly can.

REMEMBER YOUR COLLEAGUE’S NAMES

We’ve all done this – we meet someone new, and immediately forget their name. According to Forbes, there’s a simple trick to avoid this: Meet and Repeat. When someone says their name, repeat it back to them.

Magically, it will stay in your memory forever! Well, at least a little bit longer than usual.

Click here for some awesome memory tips.

ASK QUESTIONS

… And lots of them. Ask your manager, your colleagues or anyone else that can help you. You’ll not only get to know people better but the more you learn, the sooner you’ll be familiar with things, and you’ll be running like a Coltie in no time.

GET TO KNOW YOUR COLT BUDDY

On your first day, we give you a built-in friend. They’re there for you, whatever you need. They’ve been with Colt for a while, so they can and will show you the ropes.

KNOW WHERE THE KITCHEN IS

This helps you to feel like you belong. Also consider offering to get coffee or tea on behalf of a colleague or your manager. Use this as a chance to show them that you are part of the team.

YOU'VE GOT SUPPORT

Remember, you are not alone in your first week. HR is your partner and while you’re getting to know your colleagues, let them answer any questions you have and introduce you to the right people.
