LIFE IS A NEGOTIATION

Everything we do, at work or at home, involves dancing with the skill of negotiation.

Whether you’re walking down the street or driving in traffic, interacting with a colleague in the office or discussing pricing with a customer, you are negotiating.

Negotiation influences everything so the better skilled you are at it, the more successful you will be.

When two or more parties get together in discussion, they are each negotiating to find the best possible outcome to meet their different objectives.

Different outcomes are always possible. Of course, the ideal is when both parties get exactly what they want, although this isn’t always possible.

So, what are the different possible outcomes?

THE OUTCOMES

Negotiations can end in different ways.

Win-Win:
The best outcome to any negotiation is a Win-Win outcome that is mutually beneficial. This happens when both sides gain from the discussions and walk away satisfied.

Win-Lose:
When your negotiation ends and you feel satisfied but the other is unhappy with the outcome – that side is left feeling as though they've lost out.

Lose-Win:
When the outcome is reversed and you feel unsatisfied but the other is happy with the outcome – you feel as though you’ve lost out.

Lose-Lose:
When neither side ends up getting what they wanted or hoped to achieve, and both feel dissatisfied by the outcome.

GETTING TO YES

How do you get to a YES outcome with your customers?

Start by being curious. First find out everything that is available and can be offered to meet what they want.

Price may not be the only negotiating tool you have. You may be able to offer a different service level or a longer-term contract.

Either of these could be as valuable as price to both Colt and your customer.

So, be sure to prepare well so that you know everything your customer would be interested in before you even start negotiating.

Successful negotiators possess an equal amount of competitive and collaborative skills. Develop these skills to become more successful.

COMPETITIVE NEGOTIATING SKILLS

NERVE AND TENACITY

Keep calm. Hold your position and values without being negative or insulting to others.

Be confident of your facts when you make your offers.

SELF-DISCIPLINE & INSTINCT

Control the pace of the conversation and be patient so that there is time for your customer to think over your offer.

Trust yourself and your instincts.

ASSERTIVENESS

Be firm but respectful.

Ask for what you want and work hard to get it, but make sure you are respectful at all times.

COLLABORATIVE NEGOTIATING SKILLS

HUMILITY & CAUTION

Manage your ego — this will help you to earn your customer’s respect and should give you the results you’re looking for.

But remember always to listen to your internal doubts — if it seems too good to be true, it usually is!

CREATIVITY & CURIOSITY

Be creative with new and fresh ways to offer value.

Do your research and be prepared. Have the facts at your fingertips and know the details.

NUMERICAL REASONING

Don’t even think about negotiating a deal without knowing the full value of the deal.

Find the balance between gains and losses in advance so that you know how much room you have for negotiation.
