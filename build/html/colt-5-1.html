The Differentiator for Success – COMMUNICATE EFFECTIVELY

It’s easy to tell your colleagues at work to communicate effectively.

When they ask you how it is done, have these answers up your sleeve!

STRUCTURE

To structure your communication, be clear about why, what and how you deliver your message.

PREPARATION

Respect people’s time by being well-prepared for any conversations, meetings or discussions.

AUTHENTICITY

Create trust with the person receiving your message.

Be true to who you are and how you communicate. Let the tone of your voice, your ability to listen and your responses show your sincerity.

THE PURPOSE OF GOOD COMMUNICATION

Mark Beeden (a Coltie expert) explains:

Good communication is all about driving action. You’re trying to change thought processes, beliefs and behaviours so your communication needs to be aimed at communicating change.

Before you start, you should know exactly which message you want to communicate so that your information and purpose are delivered as clearly as possible.

GOOD COMMUNICATION TIPS FROM THE MONROE MODEL

Use these five steps as a technique to organise your communication in a way that inspires people into action and allows for maximum impact from your message.

1. GET ATTENTION

Grab the attention of your audience. Use storytelling, humour, shock statistics or whatever it takes, but make sure they sit up and take notice.

2. ESTABLISH THE NEED

Show your audience that there is a problem and that what is currently happening is no longer good enough. They need to make a change and take action.

3. SATISFY THE NEED

Introduce your solution to your audience. Be sure to give specifics and workable solutions to your audience’s problem.

4. VISUALISE THE FUTURE

This is where you describe to your audience what will happen if they follow your solution, or if they don’t and choose to do nothing. The closer you stick to reality, the easier it will be to motivate your audience to take action.

5. ACTION

Lastly, make sure the audience has specific details of the actions they need to take to solve the problem.
