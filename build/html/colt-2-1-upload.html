I'M A COLTIE, NOW WHAT?

You had the interview; you got the job. Now it’s your first day at Colt. A million questions run through your mind. Will I make it? Will I be successful? What first impression will I give to my new colleagues?

CONGRATULATIONS!

The first day at a new job can be difficult, and you have to find ways to fit in quickly. Making a good impression is important. These practical steps and tips are here to help you through the first week of your Colt career. Use them and be surprised at the ease of your transition!

YOUR FIRST WEEK

This week, try to balance the pressure and anxiety. It’s super exciting, although sometimes overwhelming, to meet your new colleagues, learn your new role, and find your way around the office. But, don’t sweat the small stuff and try to take in as much as you possibly can.

REMEMBER YOUR COLLEAGUES' NAMES

We’ve all done this – we meet someone new, and immediately forget their name. According to Forbes, there’s a simple trick to avoid this: Meet and Repeat. When someone says their name, repeat it back to them.

Click here for some awesome memory tips.

ASK QUESTIONS

… And lots of them. Ask your Line Manager, colleagues, or anyone else that can help you. You’ll not only get to know people better, but the more you learn, the sooner you’ll be familiar with things. Do this, and you’ll be running like a Coltie in no time.

GET TO KNOW YOUR COLT BUDDY

We give you a built-in colleague on your first day. They’re there for you, whatever you need. They have the knowledge to assist, guide and encourage you.

MAKE CONNECTIONS

This helps you to feel like you belong. Also consider offering to get coffee or tea on behalf of a colleague or your Line Manager. Use this as a chance to show them that you are part of the team.

Remember, you are not alone in your first week. HR is there to support you while you’re getting to know your colleagues, let them answer any questions you have and introduce you to the right people.

Make use of this Colt Excel platform to interact with other New Joiners and share your experiences. We are all here to help you have the smoothest transition and to become a happy, successful Coltie!
