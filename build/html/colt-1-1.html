Collaboration

The action of two or more people working together, to produce or achieve something.

We’ve all heard about it. Some do it more effectively than others. Some like working in their own bubble but, what we do know is that, strong collaboration enables creative problem-solving and greatly improves the products and services we offer our customers.

For us Colties, working with colleagues from Canada to India, China and Spain is one of the many benefits of working at Colt. But for us to work effectively, across the globe, we need to make an effort to include our colleagues, even when it may seem difficult to do so.

COHESION DESPITE DISTANCE

It isn’t always easy to stay connected to your colleagues, or even know who to bring in when certain expertise is needed. Communication is your best tool at Colt, to aid collaboration. Communication in the form of emails, online chats, bridge or Webex meetings or even just a simple telephone call to check in with your colleagues can often give you a fresh perspective.

GLOBAL PRESENCE

A global presence is a competitive advantage for Colt. Having offices and support in a wide range of countries allows us to target multinational companies, and build good customer relationships.

VALUE CREATION

When you and your colleagues share your best practices, it aids the efficiency of the team and organisation as a whole. Better team decision-making is achieved through shared knowledge and experience, which in turn leads to saving costs, and streamlining processes and tasks.

FACING CHALLENGES

The first step towards improving your collaboration skills is being aware of the possible challenges, and what to do in those instances.

Read through the section below, to learn about how to overcome some of the most common challenges.

We all face challenges when it comes to collaborating, be aware of them and find out what to do when you are in these situations.

NO DEFINED GOALS

Collaborative efforts fail without defined goals.

What to do:

Set project goals! These goals are the roadmap to successful collaboration and define the outline of who needs to be involved, and what skill sets and knowledge are needed. They also help to guide conversations and keep the focus on reaching objectives.

GEOGRAPHICAL DISTANCE

Distances between colleagues will negatively impact collaboration if you and your colleagues don’t get familiar with video conferencing technology or other virtual communication tools.

What to do:

Know the tools available to you. In today’s global world where we work with colleagues both near and far, make sure you know how best to communicate with globally.

COMPLACENCY

Watch out for collaboration which can lead to complacency when team members don’t take ownership or accountability for their work and rely on others to make it work.

What to do:

Even though you’re working with others, you must still take pride in your work, encourage your colleagues and aim to deliver products and services of the highest quality to our customers. When things go wrong, which they do, don’t blame others. rather accept responsibility as a group, including all your successes and failures.

COMPETITIVE BEHAVIOUR

Competitive, or ego-driven behaviour can block teams working together well.

What to do:

Inspire everyone around you to share their knowledge and contribute to a culture of trust and openness. When this is in place, successful collaboration is inevitable. The whole group working together will have a more powerful impact than individuals working in isolation.
