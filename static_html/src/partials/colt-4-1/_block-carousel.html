ANALYTICAL

DESCRIPTION:
They are thoughtful, serious and purposeful. They are the team members who are self-disciplined perfectionists. They rely on data to make decisions, which may take them a bit longer as they value the details.

BUILDING THE RELATIONSHIP:
It’s important to provide an analytical person with all the facts and information when asking them to finish a task. Be patient and help them with the decision-making process.

AMIABLE

DESCRIPTION:
The amiable member of your team will be patient, sympathetic and kind. They are very likeable and tend to be easy going and diplomatic.

BUILDING THE RELATIONSHIP:
Encourage them to take more risks. Work closely with them. Don’t be too hard and pushy.

DRIVER

DESCRIPTION:
Drivers are determined, decisive and confident. They work hard to finish a task and are not easily discouraged, but can sometimes appear to be insensitive and harsh. Drivers will be eager to get things done and push projects forward.

BUILDING THE RELATIONSHIP:
Drivers dislike being micromanaged so give them some responsibility and the freedom to find the best way to reach a goal or finish a task.

EXPRESSIVE

DESCRIPTION:
Your expressive team member is funny, charming and outgoing. They are ambitious and can be persuasive. Remember, they can be more productive when you let them have fun. The humour in the workplace can be great for the team.

BUILDING THE RELATIONSHIP:
Show excitement and enthusiasm. Show appreciation for their sense of humour and personality.
