1. BE READY, WILLING AND SELF-AWARE

Always be ready and willing to make a change. Take ownership of your self-coaching and use your emotional intelligence (EQ) wisely as you communicate your own emotions and deal with the emotional reactions of others around you.

2. IDENTIFY YOUR SPECIFIC INTENTION

Choose a specific behaviour that you want to change, then think about ‘how’ you are going to change it.

3. ASK FOR HELP

Handpick a mentor and people to form your ‘circle of support’ – they will guide and help you. Remember, being able to recognise and admit you need help is an important part of your personal development journey. Don’t waste time doing the wrong thing!

4. GET THEIR FEEDBACK

Ask your circle of support for their objective feedback about your behaviour.

5. LEARN FROM THEIR FEEDBACK

Be resilient in your reactions as you assess the feedback you get. You trust your circle of support, so learn from it as part of your journey to become a self-mastery guru.

6. SO SOMETHING ABOUT IT

Use your circle of support’s feedback wisely. Plan how you’re going to transform your behaviour, then implement your plan. Prioritise and execute - that’s the key!

7. MONITOR AND MAINTAIN

Ask your circle of support for their objective feedback about your behaviour.
